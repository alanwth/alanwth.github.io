Examining herding behaviour in the Chinese A&B shares market

My final year dissertation gives me the opportunity to explore around with financial data software including the Bloomberg terminal and Eikon. I have also been exposed to analysing data and running regressions with Stata. To extend my programming skills to my dissertation, I have used Python to assist with my data collection process, as well as to visualise the data.

Required tasks:

Bloomberg Terminal and Eikon

Stata

Stata is a statistical software widely used by economists, although the syntax and the logic behind Stata are different from other languages that are widely used for statistics, I believe the knowledge I acquired when using Stata as a tool for analysing data would allow me to better adapt to other statistical software.

For example, in my dissertation, I have collected data for over 80 dual-listed stocks in the Chinese A and B share market, by using Stata, I have conducted regressions based on the work of Christie and Huang (1995), as well as Chang et al. (2000). Moreover, by using Stata I was able to easily conduct time series tests to ensure the t-statistic and other OLS assumptions are valid, then also apply corrections to the problem when applicable.

Extneded tasks:

Python

To cross-validate my data, I have utilised Python to help to get data, using the Yahoo Finance API. During the process, I have learnt a lot about using different libraries that work with Excel spreadsheets, such as the Padas library.
